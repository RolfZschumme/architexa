Find cycles in your architecture

Architectural cycles can cause a number of headaches for developers. Spotting them early can save a lot of time and effort when it comes time to refactor the codebase.

To spot code cycles with Architexa first

  1. Open your project in a Layered diagram
  2. Right click anywhere in the diagram and select 'Upward Arrows' -> 'Show Always'

Now each of these upward arrows represents a connection between two packages that depend on each other, either directly, or through one or more other packages. To see more detail about these dependencies do the following:

  1. Hold down ctrl and left click on each of the packages you are interested in.
  2. Right click on one of the selected packages and choose focus from the context menu
  3. This will remove any extraneous packages and allow you to explore the relevant packages.
  4. You can then expand the packages by double clicking and continue to keep an eye out for upward connections
